From April 2012 to October 2013, I worked at IMM Lisbon on a cross-disciplinary research group in Neurocomputational Modelling [C], supervised by Professor Tiago V. Maia, PhD (Research Group Page). Project: Research in Computational Neuroscience redirects to this page

In the first few months (April-June 2012) we were introduced to neurocomputational modelling in MatLab. Vasco Conceição (a colleague from the BSc in Biomedical Engineering) and I were assigned the research project Anomalies in Habit Learning in Patients with Neuropsychiatric Diseases (in this case, ADHD).

Based on the data from a preexisting empirical trial, our goal was to study how ADHD and the corresponding disturbances in the patient’s dopaminergic system were related to the ability of learning habits. To do so, we used Reinforcement Learning algorithms (inspired in Machine Learning) to find a correlation between positive and negative feedbacks in the patient’s brain with the patient’s tendency to respond in a random or assertive manner. Once done, we tried to establish if the obtained mathematical parameters matched our initial hypothesis (based on neurophysiological studies), to increase the validity of our models and findings.
