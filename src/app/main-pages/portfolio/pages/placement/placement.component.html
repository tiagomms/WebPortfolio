*SOLEs is an acronym for Self-Organised Learning Environments. It is a model of learning in which students research and self-organize in groups with the help of computers with internet access, researching on ‘big questions’ with minimal support from outside agents (e.g. teachers) [for more information, check the SOLE Toolkit].

Placement Essay

As part of the Master’s in Education in Newcastle University [D], during a 3 week placement in Kolkata (India), I implemented SOLE sessions in an International K-12 Private School. For 2 weeks, I designed and monitored SOLE sessions from grades 3-11. During the last week, I conducted a full-day workshop on SOLEs for the school teachers, followed by 3 sessions of teacher training on starting SOLEs in their classroom.

Back in Newcastle, I presented a Powerpoint about the placement, finished a research diary and wrote an essay on: “Is schooling the best way to give education?" , which can be found attached to this page.

On this project I was supervised and counselled by Dr. James Stanfield and Dr. Sugata Mitra (TED Prize 2013) from Newcastle University’s SOLE Central, and Dr. Christopher John-Counihan from Newcastle University’s E.G. West Centre. In Kolkata I worked with Professor Dipika Rao, who provided all the conditions for my work.
