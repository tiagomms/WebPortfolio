My first education experience consisted of training monitors for the ACRIDES Summer Camp for misfortunate children in Cape Verde [B - 1]. In November 2010, I became co-responsible for the new volunteers’ training at GASTagus. Susana Lavado (my supervisor) and I created guidelines, training objectives, and researched a lot about group activities and volunteering training [B - 2].

*SOLEs is an acronym for Self-Organised Learning Environments. It is a model of learning in which students research and self-organize in groups with the help of computers with internet access, researching on ‘big questions’ with minimal support from outside agents (e.g. teachers) [for more information, check the SOLE Toolkit].

The first formal education training was during the Master’s in Education Technology at Newcastle University [D - 3]. For my thesis, I was the first to research and design SOLEs for children in Mathematics [D - 4, 5].

In February 2015, I was an invited speaker on ‘The Future of Sustainable Development Goals for Education’ for the International Development Society Conference [7] at Newcastle University.

Currently, my main subject of interest is maths education. In this field, I am inspired by Bret Victor’s Kill Math (2011); Yelena McManaman and Maria Droujkova’s Moebius Noodles (2013), a book with games for toddlers on notions of combinatorics, algebra and advanced calculus; and Papert’s work on mathematical programming languages for children. From a philosophical standpoint, I am inspired by Tagore’s ideal:

" The highest education is that which does not merely give us information but makes our life in harmony with all existence. " (Rabindranath Tagore, Personality, 1917)

Working for and with children in order to develop new ways of creating ‘empathy’ with data and information is something I would love to do in the future. I feel that the concepts I have learned in information design and education technology could be most useful in achieving this goal.
