Developed at IST Lisbon (October 2012 - January 2013) for the module Instrumentation and Signal Acquisition, a part of MSc’s in Biomedical Engineering [A]. This project’s goal was to develop a biofeedback game, in which the difficulty would increase if the user exhibited signs of anxiety or distress while playing. The user, once aware, should use breathing techniques to reduce his/hers levels of anxiety, and therefore reduce the game’s difficulty.

In order to do so, an Arduino was adapted to an Electrocardiogram circuit [ECG] and programmed to calculate the average heart rate of the user. After processing the signals, the ECG signal and current heart rate were sent to a MatLab game code (a tetris game). While playing the game, the current heart rate vs the user’s normal heart rate were displayed. If the current heart rate exceeded a given threshold, the tetris' speed would increase.

Our workgroup was comprised of 9 students. I was the group leader and MatLab lead developer, responsible for the tetris game, the ECG signal visualisation on the game, and how the user’s current heart rate affected the game’s difficulty.
